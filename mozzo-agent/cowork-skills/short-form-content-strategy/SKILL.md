# Short-Form Content Strategy System

## Core Philosophy

The ONLY thing that matters for virality is one metric: **RI (Ratio de Interés / Interest Ratio)** — the relationship between how many people see your content and how many people LIKE it. Everything in this system exists to maximize RI.

The algorithm compares your video against every other video in real-time. If your RI is higher than the average RI in your niche, your video goes viral. If it's lower, it dies. It's purely relative — a 40% RI might be viral in one niche and dead in another.

---

## FRAMEWORK 1: The Minimum Virality Threshold (UMV)

Every niche exists on a spectrum from highly specific (bottom) to completely general (top). There are 5 awareness levels:

| Level | Definition | Example (Weight Loss) |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| **0** | Doesn't know they have a problem | Doesn't realize their eating habits are unhealthy |
| **1** | Knows they have a problem | "I need to lose weight" |
| **2** | Knows how to solve it | "Caloric deficit is the answer" |
| **3** | Knows WHO can solve it | "This specific trainer is the best" |
| **4** | Knows WHICH product solves it | "This specific program is what I need" |

### The Rule
**Your content MUST target awareness level 0 or 1.** That's where 90% of the audience is. Content at levels 2-4 will NEVER go viral because too few people understand or care about it.

### Ideal Follower vs. Ideal Client
These are often DIFFERENT people:
- **Ideal Client** = The person who buys your high-ticket product (very specific)
- **Ideal Follower** = The person your viral content attracts (much broader)

Your content attracts Ideal Followers (broad audience) → You filter them through a funnel → A small % become Ideal Clients.

**Example:** A physiotherapist specializing in padel injuries:
- ❌ "How to heal a wrist sprain from padel" (Level 3-4, RI ≈ 0.5%)
- ✅ Option A: Content about padel tips (broad padel audience → some have injuries)
- ✅ Option B: Content about general pain/physiotherapy ("neck hurts? do this") → some play padel

## FRAMEWORK 2: The 5-50 Filter (Idea Validation)

Every idea MUST pass this test before production:

### Test 1: The 5-Year-Old Test
**"Can a 5-year-old understand this concept?"**
- YES → Pass ✅
- NO → Simplify or reject ❌

### Test 2: The 50/100 Test
**"If you showed this to 100 random people on the street, would at least 50 find it interesting?"**
- YES → Pass ✅
- NO → Open the niche wider ❌

### If your product/service doesn't pass 5-50:
You need to find a broader Ideal Follower, not push your Ideal Client as content. Open the niche until the content passes both filters.

## FRAMEWORK 3: Virality Calculator (Scoring System)

Score each idea on these criteria:

| Criteria | Condition | Points |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **5-Year-Old Test** | Can a child understand it? | Required (YES/NO gate) |
| **50/100 Test** | Would 50+ of 100 random people care? | Required (YES/NO gate) |
| **Viral Reference** | Does a similar video exist that went viral? | +2 if YES |
| **Viral Market (SSD+D)** | Is it in Health, Sex, Money, or Personal Dev? | +2 if YES |
| **Trending Topic** | Is it timely/seasonal/current? | +1.5 if YES |
| **Generates Controversy** | Will people argue in comments? | +2 if YES |
| **Format Multiplier** | Interview (+3), Vlog (+2.5), Talking-to-camera (+2), Selfie (+1.5), POV (+2), Dynamic (+2) | Variable |

### Scoring Thresholds
- **Below 5 points** → ❌ DO NOT PRODUCE. Bad idea.
- **5-7 points** → ⚠️ Marginal. Needs improvement.
- **7-10 points** → ✅ Good idea. Produce it.
- **Above 10 points** → 🔥 Excellent. High viral potential.

## FRAMEWORK 4: The 4 Viral Markets (SSD+D)

ALL viral content falls into one of these four markets. If your content doesn't connect to at least one, it will struggle:

1. **S — Salud (Health)**: Body, fitness, weight loss, sleep, energy, longevity, pain
2. **S — Sexo (Sex/Relationships)**: Dating, attraction, relationships, breakups, marriage
3. **D — Dinero (Money)**: Earning, saving, investing, business, side hustles, credit, wealth
4. **D — Desarrollo Personal (Personal Development)**: Habits, productivity, reading, discipline, mindset, spirituality

### Content Approach by Market

**Health**: Show the RESULT, not the science. "Lose belly fat" not "adipocyte mobilization." Target the desire (look good, feel energetic) at level 0-1. Go AGAINST popular beliefs for controversy.

**Money**: People want the MONEY, not the skill. "I made $7,600/month doing this" not "Learn this business model." Always frame around the financial result first, explain the method second.

**Sex/Relationships**: Make them realize they HAVE a problem. "If you argue with your partner more than once a week, you have a problem." Level 0-1 content that surfaces hidden issues.

**Personal Development**: Connect to tangible life improvements. "This book made me my first $3M" not "Great book about mindset."

## FRAMEWORK 5: Idea Generation (3 Sources)

### Source 1: Same Niche References (20%)
Find viral videos in the same niche/sector and reframe them:
- Same idea, different angle
- Same idea, different format
- Same idea, updated for current trends
- Use outlier detection tools to find what's already working

### Source 2: Cross-Niche Adaptation (30%)
Take viral ideas from OTHER niches and adapt them:
- A viral money hook → adapted for health
- A viral relationship format → adapted for business
- Look for universal emotional patterns that transfer

### Source 3: Original Ideas (50%)
Create from scratch using the frameworks above:
- Apply 5-50 filter
- Score with Virality Calculator
- Validate against SSD+D markets
- Test with references when possible

## FRAMEWORK 6: Video Structure (The 4-Part Script)

Every viral short-form video follows this structure:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. HOOK (0-3 seconds)                  │  ← MOST IMPORTANT. 0.6 seconds to stop the scroll.
│     Stop the scroll. Period.            │     If nobody stops, the rest doesn't matter.
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  2. STORY/CONTEXT (3-25 seconds)        │  ← Build curiosity. Personal story, credibility,
│     Keep them watching.                 │     setup the problem. DELAY the answer.
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  3. MORAL/VALUE (25-45 seconds)         │  ← Deliver the teaching, the insight, the reveal.
│     Give the payoff.                    │     Prove expertise. Give actionable value.
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  4. CTA (last 5-10 seconds)            │  ← Tell them what to do: follow, comment keyword,
│     Drive the action.                   │     check link, save this video.
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Scripting Rules:
- **NEVER reveal the answer before the midpoint.** Delay, tease, build curiosity.
- **The hook and the idea can be the same** (e.g., "How much do you spend per month in Miami?" is both hook and idea).
- **Use a RE-HOOK at 3-5 seconds** — a second line that deepens the curiosity after the initial hook.
- **Include a case study/proof** before the CTA when the objective is sales.
- **Keep total duration 45-60 seconds** for optimal performance.
- **Technical content goes in the SECOND HALF** — simple, emotional, broad content goes first.

### Script Template
```
VIDEO #: [number]
OBJECTIVE: [Viral / Sales / Followers / Branding]
WINNING IDEA: [the core idea, validated with calculator]
FORMAT: [Interview / Talking-to-camera / Selfie / Vlog / POV / Dynamic / Characters]
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT: [location, props needed, wardrobe, etc.]

HOOK: [The opening line — must pass 5-50 filter]
RE-HOOK: [Second line that deepens curiosity or adds urgency]
STORY/CONTEXT: [The setup, personal story, credibility builder]
MORAL/VALUE: [The teaching, the insight, the reveal]
CTA: [The call to action — keyword, follow, save, etc.]
```

## FRAMEWORK 7: The 50 Hook Types (Reference Library)

When writing hooks, select from these proven categories. Each hook type works differently and should be matched to the content idea and niche.

### Top-Performing Hook Categories:

**1. Gossip Hook** — Use a familiar person/place/thing to create curiosity through proximity.
- Formula: "[Famous person] does THIS with [topic], and you can do it too to [desired result]"
- Example: "Leo Messi does this with his money that anyone can do to get rich"

**2. Formula Hook** — State a desired result achieved in a specific timeframe.
- Formula: "How I [achieved result] in [timeframe]"
- Example: "How I lost 3kg of fat in one month"

**3. Negative Hook** — Appeal to loss aversion (people fear losing more than gaining).
- Formula: "If you don't know [this], you'll never [desired outcome]"
- Example: "If you don't know this viral hook, you'll never go viral on social media"

**4. Discordant Connections** — Connect two unrelated things to create confusion/curiosity.
- Formula: "What does [thing A] have to do with [thing B]?"
- Example: "What does Coca-Cola have to do with Area 51?"

**5. Once Upon a Time** — Tell a story about a transformation.
- Formula: "This [person] went from [bad situation] to [amazing result] doing one simple thing"
- Example: "This kid went from being a delivery driver to having a $67M fortune doing one thing"

**6. Challenge/Bet Hook** — Attack the ego with percentages.
- Formula: "[X]% of people do this wrong, and only [Y]% who do THIS will [result]"
- Example: "99% of people study wrong — if you do this, you'll be in the 1% who pass everything"

**7. Surprise Hook** — Physical action, sound, or unexpected visual to grab attention.
- Use objects, jumps, camera changes, something dropping — any attention-grabbing physical moment.

**8. Induced Question** — Ask a question with a seemingly obvious answer, then challenge it.
- Formula: "Don't you think it's absurd to [common behavior]?"
- Example: "Don't you think it's absurd to pay for concerts to hear songs you've already heard?"

**9. Hole Hook** — Create an information gap that can ONLY be filled by watching the full video.
- Formula: "[Person] discovered something in [place] that changed their life forever"
- Delay the reveal as long as possible.

**10. Rule of Three** — Promise 3 steps/items/secrets, hint the last one is the best.
- Formula: "The 3 best [things] to [result]. The last one is my favorite."
- Example: "The 3 best books to become a millionaire. The last one changed my life."

**11. Famous Quote** — Use a recognized person's quote for familiarity and authority.
- Formula: "[Famous person] said: '[quote]'"

**12. Common Enemy** — Blame an external factor for the audience's problem.
- Formula: "If you can't [achieve result], it's not your fault — it's because of [external enemy]"
- Example: "If you can't lose weight, it's not because you don't exercise — it's because of this thing nobody told you about"

**13. Controversy/Against Popular Belief** — State something that contradicts what everyone believes.
- Formula: "[Common belief] is completely wrong. Here's why."
- Example: "Doing crunches does NOT give you abs. Here's what actually works."

**14. Easy Hook** — Frame something perceived as hard as easy.
- Formula: "Losing weight is actually ridiculously easy" / "[Hard thing] is too simple if you do this"

**15. Hypothetical Hook** — "If tomorrow I woke up with [problem], this is exactly what I'd do"
- Positions you as the expert with a concrete action plan.

**16. What Do You Prefer?** — Give a polarizing choice (great for interview format).
- Formula: "What do you prefer: [option A] or [option B]?"

**17. Offensive Hook** — Attack the viewer's ego directly (they watch to see if they should be offended).
- Formula: "If you're [negative state], it's because of THIS reason"
- Example: "If you're broke, it's because you don't do this"

**18. Hack/Tutorial Hook** — Promise a quick, actionable shortcut.
- Formula: "Hack to [achieve result] in [short timeframe]"

**19. After Hook** — Lead with experience/credentials, then give advice.
- Formula: "After [impressive achievement], these are the only [X] things I'd recommend"

**20. The Only Way Hook** — Present your solution as the singular path.
- Formula: "The ONLY way to [result] is [method]" / "The best way to [result] is [counterintuitive method]"

**21. Visual Hook** — Direct attention to a physical object or location on screen.
- Formula: "This [object] is going to teach you [valuable lesson]"

## FRAMEWORK 8: Video Formats

Choose the format BEFORE writing the script, as it affects structure:

| Format | Description | Best For | Virality Potential |
|--------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Interview** | Two people, one asks questions | Sales CTAs, credibility, entertainment | Highest (less saturated) |
| **Talking to Camera** | Direct to camera, expert delivery | Education, authority, sales | High |
| **Selfie** | Casual, phone-in-hand | Quick tips, reactions, day-in-life | Medium |
| **Vlog/Blog** | Moving, showing environment | Lifestyle, demonstrations, storytelling | High |
| **POV** | First-person perspective | Immersive experiences, demonstrations | High |
| **Dynamic** | Multiple format changes within one video | Complex ideas, high retention | High |
| **Voice Over** | Narration over B-roll footage | Educational, cinematic storytelling | Medium-High |
| **Characters** | Playing different roles/personas | Comedy, comparisons, skits | Medium-High |

### Format Rules:
- **Interview format scores highest** in the Virality Calculator because it's less saturated
- **Never use the same format for every video** — variety keeps the audience engaged
- **Match format to content**: street interviews for man-on-street questions, vlog for lifestyle/demonstrations, talking-to-camera for expert authority

## FRAMEWORK 10: Pre-Production Checklist

Before ANY recording session, verify:
- [ ] Props/objects needed for the visual hooks (credit cards, food, books, etc.)
- [ ] Location scouted and confirmed (clean, good lighting, matches the content concept)
- [ ] Client briefed on the script and key talking points
- [ ] Client has reviewed and filled in expert-knowledge gaps in the script
- [ ] Wardrobe/styling prepared if needed
- [ ] Equipment ready (microphone type, camera angles, lighting)
- [ ] Clear visual of HOW the video should look when you close your eyes and imagine it

**The #1 failure point is poor recording preparation, not bad ideas or scripts.**

## FRAMEWORK 11: Instagram Stories Strategy

Stories serve a DIFFERENT purpose than Reels:
- **Reels** = Cast the fishing net (attract new followers, go viral)
- **Stories** = Connect with the catch (nurture, build trust, sell)

### Stories Rules:
- Keep them NATURAL — minimal editing, no heavy effects
- Stories are for CONNECTION, not stimulation
- People enter Stories AFTER consuming high-dopamine Reels — they want something human

### Story Sequence Structure (7-12 stories per sequence):
1. **Lifestyle/Flex** — Show environment, results, social proof (1-2 stories)
2. **Context/Value** — Explain something useful, provide insight (2-3 stories)
3. **Interaction** — Poll, question, or engagement prompt (1 story)
4. **Results/Proof** — Show transformations, testimonials, case studies (1-2 stories)
5. **Scarcity/Urgency** — Limited spots, demand, DM screenshots (1 story)
6. **CTA** — "Comment [keyword]" / "DM me [word]" (1 story)
7. **Humor/Cooldown** — End with something light to avoid feeling salesy (1 story)

### Story Content by Niche:
- **Money**: Show income results, lifestyle upgrades, client wins, behind-the-scenes of work
- **Health**: Show meal prep, workout clips, before/after, daily routine
- **Sex/Relationships**: Use analogies/metaphors (nothing explicit), show connection, address common problems
- **Personal Development**: Show reading, routines, discipline moments, transformation stories

### 3 Best Interaction Methods:
1. **Polarizing Question** — Ask something that makes people pick sides
2. **DM Screenshots** — Show real messages from followers/clients to create belonging
3. **Direct Question + Response Feature** — Ask, collect answers, feature them in next stories

### The Sales Sequence Formula:
Create **repressed demand** (show people wanting to work with you) → Create **scarcity** (limited spots) → Let THEM ask YOU → CTA with keyword → Cooldown with humor

## HOW TO USE THIS SKILL

### When Generating Ideas:
1. Identify the niche and which SSD+D market it belongs to
2. Determine the awareness level of the target audience
3. Generate ideas at Level 0-1 awareness
4. Run each idea through the 5-50 Filter
5. Score with the Virality Calculator
6. Only produce ideas scoring 7+

### When Validating Ideas:
1. Apply the 5-50 Filter (both tests must pass)
2. Score with Virality Calculator
3. Check for viral references (same idea that already worked)
4. Verify it targets awareness Level 0-1
5. Confirm it connects to an SSD+D market
6. Return verdict: PRODUCE / REVISE / SKIP with score and reasoning

### When Writing Scripts:
1. Start with the Winning Idea (already validated)
2. Choose the Format
3. Select a Hook Type from the library that matches
4. Write following the 4-part structure: Hook → Story/Context → Moral/Value → CTA
5. Apply the delay-the-reveal principle
6. Include a re-hook after the main hook
7. Put technical/expert content in the second half only
8. End with a clear CTA that matches the video's objective

### When Reviewing Scripts:
Check against this rubric:

| Criteria | Check |
|----------|-------|
| Does the hook stop the scroll in 0.6 seconds? | ☐ |
| Does the idea pass the 5-50 filter? | ☐ |
| Is the content at awareness Level 0-1? | ☐ |
| Is the reveal/answer delayed past the midpoint? | ☐ |
| Does it connect to an SSD+D market? | ☐ |
| Is there a re-hook after the initial hook? | ☐ |
| Is technical content saved for the second half? | ☐ |
| Is there a clear CTA that matches the objective? | ☐ |
| Does the format match the content type? | ☐ |
| Would this generate controversy or comments? | ☐ |
| Is there a case study/proof element (if sales objective)? | ☐ |
| Is total duration 45-60 seconds? | ☐ |

Provide specific feedback on each failing criterion with a suggested fix.

### When Analyzing Competitor Content:
1. Identify the hook type used (from the 50 Hook Library)
2. Identify the awareness level targeted
3. Score it with the Virality Calculator retroactively
4. Extract the replicable formula (what can be adapted, not copied)
5. Note the format used and whether a different format could work better
6. Identify if it goes against popular belief (controversy factor)

## IMPORTANT PRINCIPLES

1. **Never copy. Adapt.** Understand WHY something works, then apply the principle to your niche.
2. **The hook is everything.** If nobody stops scrolling, the best content in the world is worthless.
3. **Simple beats complex.** Always. At every stage. In every niche.
4. **Controversy drives engagement.** Going against popular beliefs is one of the most reliable virality triggers.
5. **People want results, not methods.** Frame around the OUTCOME ("I made $7,600") not the process ("Learn this business model").
6. **Your content is a product.** Think of every video as lead generation for a sales funnel, not "posting content."
7. **The client fills the expertise gaps.** The strategist provides structure, hooks, and format. The expert provides the technical value. Never fake expertise with AI-generated content as the base.
8. **Preparation > Everything.** The #1 reason recordings fail is poor preparation, not bad ideas.
